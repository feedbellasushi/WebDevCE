Who Were the Signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A strong advocate for independence. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and thinker. Helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress. Known for his bold, oversized signatureâ€”a symbol of defiance.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Key organizer of resistance to British rule (Boston Tea Party). Cousin of John Adams.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four major founding documents: Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer. Last surviving signer, died in 1832.
